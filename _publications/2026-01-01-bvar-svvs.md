excerpt: 'With Kyu Ho Kang. This paper develops a Bayesian vector autoregression with stochastic-volatility variable selection (BVAR-SVVS) that determines, variable by variable, whether each series is better characterized by a stochastic-volatility or constant-volatility specification. The model uses a spike-and-slab prior on the innovation variance of each variable''s log-volatility process within a hierarchical Minnesota prior framework. This approach avoids the combinatorial burden of explicit model comparison while remaining computationally feasible in large systems. Using U.S. quarterly macroeconomic datasets of increasing dimension, we find marked heterogeneity in volatility dynamics across variables. Real activity variables are systematically assigned to stochastic-volatility specifications, whereas financial and price-related series more often favor constant volatility. Relative to Bayesian VARs that impose either stochastic volatility or constant volatility uniformly across all variables, the proposed approach delivers sizable gains in density forecasting. The improvements are strongest in large systems and at medium-to-long forecast horizons.'
